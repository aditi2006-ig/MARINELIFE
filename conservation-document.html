About Marine Life Conservation

Marine life conservation is crucial to maintaining the health of our oceans. Protecting the diverse ecosystems and creatures that inhabit our seas is not only vital for biodiversity, but also for the balance of life on Earth.Marine conservation is also known as ocean conservation. The health of all life on Earth depends (directly or indirectly) on a healthy ocean. As humans began to realize their increasing impacts on the ocean, the field of marine conservation arose in response. This article discusses the definition of marine conservation, techniques used in the field, and some of the most important ocean conservation issues.

Man has become by far the greatest predator of all time. As populations mount and land-grown food supplies are unable to feed the growing numbers of the hungry, man is turning more and more to the sea for his food. On land man has slowly learned to conserve the soil lest it stop producing crops. But on the ocean, man is a hunter only. He takes but returns little. If the bounty of the sea is not to be exhausted, man must learn to farm it as he farms the land, by sowing as well as reaping. – The Ocean World of Jacques Cousteau, 1975

Why Marine Life Conservation Matters!!!

Why Marine Life Conservation Matters!!!

Marine life contributes to the global ecosystem in countless ways, from maintaining oxygen levels to providing food for millions of people. Without healthy marine ecosystems, our planet's future is in jeopardy.
Marine conservation work can be done by enforcing and creating laws, such as the Endangered Species Act and Marine Mammal Protection Act. It can also be done by establishing marine protected areas, studying populations through conducting stock assessments and mitigating human activities with the goal of restoring populations.

    Marine Conservation Issues

    • Ocean acidification

    • Climate change and warming ocean temperatures

    • Sea level rise

    • Marine debris and the issue of plastics in the ocean

    • Oil spills (an issue the public became well aware of thanks to the Exxon Valdez and Deepwater Horizon spills)

    • Studying and protecting endangered species ( North Atlantic right whale, vaquita, sea turtles, monk seals and many other threatened and endangered species)

    • Dealing with the problem of shark finning

    • Regulating whaling

    • Reducing bycatch in marine fisheries and entanglements in fishing gear
