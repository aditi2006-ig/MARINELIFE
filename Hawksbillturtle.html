HAWKSBILL TURTLE




Hawksbill sea turtles inhabit the tropical and sub-tropical waters of all of the world’s major oceans. Hawksbills get their name from their unique beak-like mouth, which resembles that of a hawk and is perfect for finding food sources in hard-to-reach cracks and crevices. They are the only species of sea turtle that can survive on a diet consisting mainly of sponges. Hawksbill turtles play a key role in the function of marine ecosystems. The Hawksbill Turtle is a critically endangered sea turtle primarily found in tropical oceans. It has a distinctive beak-like mouth and beautiful, patterned shells often sought after for jewelry. These turtles play a crucial role in maintaining the health of coral reefs.

ABOUT HAWKSBILL TURTLE

In many parts of the world, hawksbills face the unique threat of being hunted for their beautiful shell, also known as “tortoise shell”, which is used by craftspeople to create many types of jewelry and trinkets. The historical hunting and killing of hawksbills for their shell nearly drove the species to extinction. Today, the Convention on International Trade of Endangered Species (CITES) forbids the trade of any turtle products on the international market, including hawksbill tortoise shell, but illegal hunting continues to represent a threat to the species in many parts of the world. The hawksbill is biofluorescent and is the first reptile recorded with this characteristic.

Where Hawksbill Turtles Are Found

Hawksbill turtles are primarily found in tropical and subtropical oceans, with key populations in the Caribbean, the Indian Ocean, and the Pacific Ocean. These turtles prefer coral reefs and the shallow waters of coastal areas where they find abundant food sources.

Did you know? Hawksbill turtles can be found in over 100 countries and territories around the world, including tropical coral reefs and coastal zones.
