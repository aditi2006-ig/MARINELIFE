Kelp Forests

Kelp forests are underwater ecosystems formed by dense growths of kelp, a type of large brown algae. These forests are among the most productive and dynamic ecosystems on Earth, providing habitat and food for countless marine species.

Found in nutrient-rich waters with cool temperatures, kelp forests thrive along coastlines worldwide. They play a critical role in maintaining biodiversity, supporting species like sea otters, fish, and invertebrates. Additionally, kelp forests help reduce coastal erosion by buffering wave energy.

However, kelp forests face threats from climate change, overfishing, and pollution. Conservation efforts are vital to protect these underwater treasures and ensure the health of marine ecosystems for generations to come.
