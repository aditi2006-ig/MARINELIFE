Mangrove Forest

Mangrove forests are unique ecosystems that grow in coastal intertidal zones, where land and sea meet.

Mangrove forests are found in tropical and subtropical regions, along coasts and estuaries in 123 countries.

Mangrove trees are adapted to survive in salty conditions and are often rooted in water. There are around 80 different species of mangrove trees. Features Mangrove forests are characterized by their dense tangle of prop roots that make the trees appear to be standing on stilts above the water.
